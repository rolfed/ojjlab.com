Jiu Jitsu

The gentle art of Brazilian Jiu Jitsu at Oregon Jiu Jitsu Lab

Master the Art of Leverage

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is a martial art and combat sport that focuses on ground fighting and submission holds. It emphasizes technique, leverage, and strategy over brute strength, making it effective for practitioners of all sizes and ages.

What You'll Learn

  • Fundamental positions and transitions
  • Submission techniques (chokes and joint locks)
  • Escape and defense strategies
  • Guard passing and retention
  • Takedowns and throws
  • Self-defense applications

Benefits

  • Improved physical fitness and flexibility
  • Enhanced problem-solving skills
  • Increased confidence and mental toughness
  • Stress relief and mental clarity
  • Strong community and friendships
  • Practical self-defense skills

Class Structure

  • Warm-up and mobility exercises
  • Technique instruction and drilling
  • Positional sparring (specific situations)
  • Live rolling (free sparring)
  • Cool-down and recovery

What to Expect

Your First Class

Arrive 15 minutes early to meet the instructor and get oriented. You'll start with basic movements and positions, working with experienced students who will help guide you.

What to Wear

For your first class, wear comfortable athletic clothes (t-shirt and shorts/athletic pants). As you progress, you'll want to invest in a gi (uniform) and other training gear.

Progression

BJJ uses a belt ranking system from white to black belt. Progression is based on technical knowledge, sparring ability, and time on the mat. Most students earn their first stripe in 3-6 months.

Community

You'll become part of a supportive community of practitioners who help each other improve. The relationships built on the mat often extend far beyond training.
