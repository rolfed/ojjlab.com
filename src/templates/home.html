We believe students grow fastest when they learn by solving problems, not by memorizing random moves.

Why We Exist

Our curriculum is built on a constraint-based approach. That means students spend more time training in real situations, where techniques have purpose and pressure.

Instead of rehearsing steps they'll forget, they learn skills that show up in live training and competition.

We've seen this approach help students of all levels — kids, adults, and competitors — make progress faster, with confidence that carries beyond the mats.

Programs

Train Smarter. Get Stronger. Build Confidence.

Jiu Jitsu

Kids (ages 5+) + Adults

Jiu Jitsu is a grappling art where the goal is to achieve a dominant position and apply submissions. Submissions are techniques such as chokes and joint locks that give smaller, skilled practitioners the ability to overcome larger opponents. Our kids' and adult classes build both technical skill and confidence on the mats.

Wrestling

Kids (ages 5+) + Adults

Wrestling focuses on takedowns, control, and dominant positioning. It is one of the oldest combat sports and a foundation for modern grappling arts. Our classes teach explosive movement, balance, and control — skills that benefit both kids learning discipline and adults looking to sharpen their grappling game.

Kickboxing

Adults

Kickboxing is a striking art that combines punches, kicks, knees, and footwork. Training builds power, speed, and conditioning while teaching practical self-defense skills. Our program is designed for adults who want to improve fitness and develop effective stand-up striking.

Competition Team

Kids (ages 5+) + Adults

For students who want to test themselves in tournaments, our competition team provides advanced training and coaching. Practices focus on systems, strategy, and high-intensity drilling with training partners who match your drive. Built for kids and adults ready to push their limits.

Instructors

Adam Fox

Head Coach

Adam Fox is a 2nd degree Black Belt under Eric Hemphill with more than 24 years of Jiu Jitsu experience. He has coached at the highest levels, including serving as head coach for the Kazakhstan National Jiu Jitsu Team and Golden Eagle Jiu Jitsu and Wrestling in Kyrgyzstan.

Adam's coaching focuses on clear systems, efficient learning, and developing both the body and mind. His students include IBJJF World Champions and UFC fighters, proof of his ability to create success at every level.

At Oregon Jiu Jitsu Lab, Adam brings his extensive experience to help each student, from kids to competitors, grow faster and smarter on the mats.

Danniel Rolfe

Coach

Danniel is a Brown Belt under Professor Eric Hemphill, with Adam Fox also playing an important role in his development as a coach and practitioner. His journey has taken him from São Paulo to Oregon, giving him a broad perspective on the art.

For more than eight years, Danniel has taught students of all levels with an approachable style that makes Jiu Jitsu accessible to everyone. His focus is on helping each student grow while continuing his own path as both a teacher and lifelong student of the art.

Justin Koreis

Justin has more than 20 years of experience in striking arts, with a strong background in kickboxing. He began in the competitive Taekwondo circuit before moving to full-contact kickboxing in 2005 under UFC veteran Ivan Salaverry, where he trained students from beginners to professional fighters.
